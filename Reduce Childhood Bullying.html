REDUCE CHILDHOOD BULLYING

Childhood Bullying
Bullying is any form of mistreatment done by an individual to a child in any setting. Childhood bullying involves, a bully using the victim's vulnerability such as age, gender, or social status to harm or intimidate him or her.
Research carried by Newman‐Carlson & Horne shows that, children in the age bracket of 2 and 12 years are exposed to school bullying by either their agemates or older ones. This kind of childhood bullying can either be verbal, social, physical or cyber. However, it does not matter which type of bullying an individual is exposed to, any form of bullying influences the educational and physical well-being of the child.
According to Craig &Pepler, childhood bullying has negative effects in the life a child. For example, several suicidal cases have been reported due to childhood bullying. Also, bullying makes a child grow with a negative attitude towards other people. Therefore, several policies and regulations have already been put in place to curb childhood bullying. But, it is sad to note that in several parts of the country, bullying is rampant. However, there are some ways which if fully implemented, can reduce bullying rates among children.
Ways to reduce childhood bullying
1. Coaching the child how to handle teasing and bullying
Usually, bullies begin with a verbal harrassment, and how the victim responds determines the next step of the bully. Bullies use the verbal harrasment to determine the weaknesses or the strengths of the child therefore, coaching kids to reply firmly and confidently in such situtions may keep the bully away. Also when the child is responding to the bully, s/he should look to directly to the bully's eyes thus scaring him/her away.
2. Talk about bullying
Children have a tendency of being reluctant to talk about bullying especially if they are the bullies. However, as an elder, it is possible to identify weird characteristics exhibited by children and talk about them. Some of the common characteristics exhibited by a bully child include:

  • Lack of empathy
  • Underdeveloped social and interpersonal skills
  • A child is looking for ways to stay popular and powerful
  • S/he excludes other kids from playing or studying with him or her
After indentifying such characteristics, it becomes possible to talk about the effects of bullying to the child. Enligntening the child about effects and consequences of childhood bullying will prevent the child from bullying others.
3. Modelling positive social behavior among children
The most effective way to keep children from being bullied, and from becoming bullies, is to make sure they grow up in loving relationships. Perkins succinctly put it, “Don’t worry that your children never listen to you. Worry that they are always watching you.” Children learn both sides of every relationship, and they can act either one. Model positive parenting. If the parent's discipline methods use power over the child, s/he will learn to use power over others, or to let others use power over him.
4. Teaching social skills to children
Palladino et al. suggests that, kids need to know they can get their needs met while being respectful of other people. Role playing with a child about how join a game at the playground, introduce themselves to another child at a party, or initiate a play date/sleepover. Kids who are successful in joining groups of kids usually observe first, and find a way to fit into the group, rather than just barging in. Make games out of social skills and practice at home. Also a parent should encourage the child to join groups and clubs that s/he enjoys.
